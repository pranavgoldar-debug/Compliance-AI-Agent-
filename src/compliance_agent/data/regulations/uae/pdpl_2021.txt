UAE FEDERAL DECREE-LAW NO. 45 OF 2021 — PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION LAW (PDPL)

Article 4. Cases of personal data processing without consent
Personal data may be processed without the data subject's consent where
processing is necessary to protect public interest, fulfill obligations
under existing laws, perform a contract to which the data subject is a
party, protect the data subject's interests, or for purposes of
preventive or occupational medicine.

Article 6. Conditions for consent to process personal data
Consent shall be a specific, clear, simple, unambiguous and easy-to-access
statement that the data subject agrees to the processing of his or her
personal data. The controller shall be able to demonstrate that the data
subject has consented. The data subject may withdraw consent at any time.

Article 7. General controller obligations
The controller shall take appropriate technical and organisational
measures and apply appropriate standards and rules to protect personal
data. The controller shall maintain a special record of personal data and
make it available to the UAE Data Office on request.

Article 9. Processor obligations
The controller shall not engage a processor unless the processor provides
sufficient guarantees that the processing shall be carried out in
accordance with the provisions of this Decree-Law. Processing carried out
by a processor shall be governed by a contract that binds the processor
to the controller.

Article 13. Right to access information
The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller,
without charge, the methods used in processing personal data, the
purposes of processing, the categories of personal data being processed,
the recipients, the period of personal data retention, and information
related to cross-border transfers.

Article 16. Right to limit, stop or erase processing
The data subject shall have the right to restrict the processing of his
or her personal data in specified cases and to request the controller to
erase his or her personal data where the personal data is no longer
necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or where the data
subject withdraws his or her consent.

Article 21. Cross-border transfer of personal data
Personal data may be transferred outside the State to jurisdictions that
have a special legislation on personal data protection providing an
adequate level of protection. Transfers to jurisdictions without such
legislation shall be permitted only on the basis of approved binding
agreements, contractual clauses or with the express consent of the data
subject.

Article 23. Reporting personal data breaches
The controller shall notify the UAE Data Office of any breach of personal
data immediately upon becoming aware of the breach, where the breach
would prejudice the privacy, confidentiality or security of the personal
data of the data subject. The notification shall include a description of
the breach and the measures taken.
